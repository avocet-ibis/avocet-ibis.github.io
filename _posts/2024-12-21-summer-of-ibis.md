---
title: Summer of the Ibis
---

The White-faced Ibis is a familiar friend to birders throughout Southern Wyoming. Throughout summer, hundreds can be seen nesting in the bulrush and foraging in the shallow alkaline waters of Hutton Lake National Wildlife Refuge, located southwest of Laramie, WY. These irridescent waders shimmer metallic green and bronze, stand tall on bright red legs, carry long, decurved bills, and, during the breeding season, sport bright pink patches of bare skin bordered by white feathers.
