---
title: Summer of the Ibis
---

The White-faced Ibis is a familiar friend to birders throughout Southern Wyoming. During summer, hundreds can be seen nesting in the bulrush and foraging along the shallow alkaline waters of Hutton Lake National Wildlife Refuge, located 10 miles southwest of Laramie, WY. These waders stand tall on a pair of red legs and carry a long, decurved bill to probe mud for food. They appear black from a distance, but when close up, their plumage transforms into a deep glossy red, and their iridescent wings shimmer metallic green and bronze. During the breeding season, adults sport bright pink patches of bare skin in front of their red eyes, bordered by a narrow line of white feathers (hence the name).

![White-faced Ibis]({{ site.baseurl }}/images/2024-12-21/white-faced.jpg "White-faced Ibis")









When near a nesting colony like the one at Hutton Lake, you will frequently see Ibis flying between their feeding grounds and nests, often calling with low croaking and grunting vocalizations.

 While in flight, they extend both their necks and legs, similar to a Sandhill Crane.
